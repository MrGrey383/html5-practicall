Personal Interests & Hobbies

My interests outside of computer science contribute to my perspective as a developer and help me stay creative and motivated.

Photography & Visual Storytelling

I'm passionate about capturing Kenya's beautiful landscapes and urban scenes. Photography has taught me about composition, visual hierarchy, and user attention - skills that directly translate to web design.

What I love about photography:

  • Technical precision: Similar to coding, photography requires attention to detail and technical understanding
  • Accessibility perspective: I write detailed alt text for my photos, considering how to describe visual content meaningfully
  • Cultural documentation: Capturing diverse communities helps me understand different user perspectives
  • Visual communication: Learning to tell stories through images informs my approach to user interface design

My photography work emphasizes the same principles I apply to web development: accessibility, inclusivity, and meaningful representation of diverse experiences.

Community Technology Education

I volunteer with local organizations to teach basic computer skills and digital literacy to underserved communities in Nairobi. This work directly influences my commitment to accessible web development.

Programs I'm Involved With

  • Senior Digital Inclusion: Teaching elderly community members how to use smartphones and access government services online
  • Youth Coding Workshops: Introducing high school students to programming concepts and career opportunities in tech
  • Women in Tech Mentorship: Supporting female students pursuing STEM education
  • Accessibility Awareness: Educating local businesses about making their digital presence more inclusive

These experiences have shown me firsthand how poor website design can exclude people from accessing essential services, reinforcing my commitment to universal design principles.

"No one will be left behind means ensuring that the furthest behind are reached first."

Language Learning & Cultural Exchange

I'm currently improving my Swahili fluency and learning basic French. Language learning has taught me about diverse communication patterns and cultural contexts that inform inclusive design.

How language learning connects to web development:

  • Internationalization awareness: Understanding how different languages affect layout and user experience
  • Cultural sensitivity: Learning about diverse communication styles and visual preferences
  • Accessibility considerations: Understanding how screen readers handle different languages and scripts
  • Content strategy: Appreciating the importance of clear, simple language for global audiences

This linguistic perspective helps me create more inclusive digital experiences that work across cultural and linguistic boundaries.

Outdoor Activities & Nature Connection

Kenya's incredible natural beauty provides endless opportunities for hiking, nature walks, and outdoor exploration. These activities help me maintain mental clarity and creative energy for problem-solving.

Favorite outdoor activities:

  1. Weekend hiking: Exploring the Ngong Hills, Mount Kenya region, and local nature trails
  2. Urban photography walks: Discovering interesting architecture and street scenes in Nairobi
  3. Community gardening: Participating in local urban agriculture initiatives
  4. Beach visits: Occasional trips to the Kenyan coast for relaxation and inspiration

These outdoor experiences provide perspective and remind me that technology should serve human needs and connect us with the natural world, not isolate us from it.

What Drives Me Forward

Personal Mission Statement

My commitment: To use technology as a bridge, not a barrier, connecting people to opportunities and information regardless of their abilities, economic status, or cultural background.

Every line of code I write, every person I teach, and every project I complete is guided by this principle. Technology should serve humanity's diverse needs, not the other way around.

This means:

  • Always considering accessibility from the beginning, not as an afterthought
  • Creating content that works across different languages, cultures, and contexts
  • Sharing knowledge freely and mentoring others who want to join the tech community
  • Building bridges between traditional communities and digital opportunities

This mission statement guides all my personal and professional decisions.
